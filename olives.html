All About Olives

Olives are one of the oldest cultivated fruits in the world. They represent resilience, patience, and Mediterranean culture. From olive trees and groves to extra virgin olive oil, olives connect nature, food, health, and history.

Origin and history

Olive cultivation goes back thousands of years across the Mediterranean region. Ancient civilizations valued olive trees for food, medicine, and trade.

Tree and growth

Olive trees are long-living and drought-resistant. They grow slowly, but with care they can produce fruit for generations and become iconic landscape trees.

From fruit to oil

Olives are harvested at different ripeness levels for different flavors. Extra virgin olive oil is made by mechanical extraction and preserves aroma and nutrients.

Taste profiles

Different varieties create different tastes: fruity, buttery, peppery, grassy, or nutty. Processing methods also shape final flavor and texture.

Health and nutrition

Olives and olive oil contain healthy fats and antioxidants. They are central to Mediterranean-style diets and often associated with balanced, heart-friendly nutrition.

Cultural symbol

The olive branch is a symbol of peace and continuity. In many cultures, olive trees represent endurance, wisdom, and a deep connection to place.
